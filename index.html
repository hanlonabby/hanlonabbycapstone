How Live Events Create Cultural Unity Through Digital Marketing

A case study by Abby Hanlon

Overview

The history of the Super Bowl Halftime Show is defined by a long-standing battle over whether it is “a break from the action” or “the main attraction,” depending on whom you ask. In the lens of communicators, the Super Bowl Halftime Show is a heavily anticipated event: featuring strategic marketing campaigns, partnerships, and global superstars at the forefront of the 50-yard line. Since the 90s, the Super Bowl Halftime Show has hosted some of the biggest names in music through strategic partnerships with major, globally recognized brands, driving year-over-year viewership worldwide.

Hypothesis

The Apple Music Super Bowl Halftime Show uses storytelling, digital integration, and Apple’s larger brand ecosystem to connect diverse audiences through one unified cultural moment.

Apple as a Brand

Apple’s Brand Identity

For 50 years, Apple has been universally recognized as “the greatest product company in history” (Jason Aten, Inc., 2026) and as one that learns from itself, its audiences, and the way the world evolves and adapts to new technology every day. The company prides itself on building products that help everyday people create, think, and perform better. Each rendition of iPhone, MacBook, iPad, Apple Watch, and more is better, more advanced, and technically sound than the last.

Apple’s target market spans the entire world: limiting no age, background, social standing, culture, gender, or occupation. Built on technology that makes a difference, Apple stands out as a globally appreciated brand that celebrates and serves all consumers.

Taken from Apple’s Values page on its website, the brand stands by this statement:

“We are committed to demonstrating that business can and should be a force for good. Achieving that takes innovation, collaboration, and a focus on serving others. It also means leading with our values in the technology we make, the way we make it, and how we treat people and the planet we share. We’re always working to leave the world better than we found it, and to create powerful tools that empower others to do the same.” (Apple Investor Relations, 2026)

Shot on iPhone

Apple is globally recognized as a leader in marketing because it doesn’t just advertise products – it communicates ideas that resonate culturally. Over the decades, Apple has evolved its approach in step with how people use technology, shifting from cinematic storytelling to simple, visual demonstrations of real-world use. This human-first strategy allows Apple to create marketing that feels intuitive, emotional, and culturally relevant – especially within live event spaces where storytelling drives engagement.

This approach continues in more recent campaigns like “Shot on iPhone”, one of the highest-performing advertisement campaigns in the world since 2015, with 70 million social media interactions and 100,000+ photo submissions (Get Kard, 2026), which showcase real, high-quality content created on Apple devices rather than relying on traditional ads.

By turning user output into the advertisement itself, Apple reinforces authenticity and inclusiveness while scaling globally – mirroring how live events rely on real experiences and audience participation. This evolution highlights why Apple remains a globally respected advertiser: it consistently turns product capability into culture, not just promotion.

Apple Music artist spotlight

Apple Music

Those same standards of authenticity are represented and celebrated even more boldly through Apple’s sub-brand: Apple Music.

Since its launch in 2015, Apple Music has pioneered the music streaming industry as the top artist-centric and music-only streaming service available to consumers, introduced as “an exciting way for fans to connect with artists” by Eddy Cue, Apple’s senior vice president of Internet Software and Services, at the time of its release. (Apple Newsroom, 2015).

Before the days of music streaming, Apple had already served as the unsung hero in music marketing strategy, dating back to the early 2000s. The company’s initiative to advertise the iconic iPod silhouette campaigns while implementing tracks from artists’ music available for purchase on iTunes had leveraged Apple as an innovator in creativity and connection from the start.

Leveraging music as a key part of Apple’s brand identity only strengthens its connection with a more diverse audience. Music provides emotional impact in ways that technology will never be able to completely match – appealing to all demographics all over the world, strengthening brand loyalty with its broader audience.
